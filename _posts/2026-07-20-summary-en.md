## [Gboard developing sign-to-text feature using camera](https://www.androidauthority.com/gboard-sign-to-text-3688910/) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Gboard version 17.8.3 beta includes a new Sign-to-Text input option that uses the phone's camera to capture sign language gestures and converts them to text via a hybrid on-device and cloud AI model. This feature represents a major accessibility breakthrough, enabling deaf or hard-of-hearing users to communicate more naturally via text input. It leverages Google DeepMind's SignGemma model and could set a new standard for inclusive technology. Only raw gesture data is sent to the cloud, not the actual video, ensuring privacy. The feature is still in early beta and not yet functional, with potential device restrictions and initial support likely limited to American Sign Language.
> **Reason**: Android Authority独家报道Google Gboard即将支持手语转文字功能，利用摄像头和云端AI识别手势，是一项重要的无障碍技术创新，对聋哑人群有深远影响。

## [US politicians optimize online presence to sway AI chatbots](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/19/us/politics/chatbots-political-campaigns.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

U.S. campaign teams are adjusting websites and publishing Q&As to influence how AI chatbots like ChatGPT describe candidates, giving rise to a new 'answer engine optimization' industry. This trend shows AI is becoming a key information source for voters, and manipulating chatbot responses could undermine election integrity. It also opens the door for foreign actors to exploit similar tactics. Research indicates that new content on Wikipedia can be ingested by chatbots in about 12 minutes, and over one-third of AI answers in a Scottish election experiment contained errors.
> **Reason**: 报道了美国政客通过优化网络内容来影响AI聊天机器人对候选人的评价，揭示了AI在政治信息传播中的新应用和潜在操纵风险，具有重要的社会影响。
