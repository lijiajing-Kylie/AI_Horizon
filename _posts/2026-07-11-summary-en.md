## [OpenAI, Google Provide AI Models to Blacklisted Chinese Firms via Singapore Units](https://www.ft.com/content/5d6aafa1-5d47-4585-aa95-6ec06a6cd20f) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The Financial Times reports that OpenAI and Google have been providing advanced AI services to Singapore subsidiaries of Alibaba, Baidu, and Tencent, whose parent companies are on the U.S. Department of Defense's 1260H list of Chinese military companies. This practice highlights a loophole in current U.S. export controls, potentially undermining restrictions on AI technology transfer to China, and has reignited Washington debates on tightening AI model exports. OpenAI last month suspended API access for a user linked to Alibaba after detecting suspected model distillation, and reported the incident to the U.S. government. In contrast, Anthropic has a stricter policy fully banning access by Chinese companies and their overseas entities.

## [EU Rules Meta's Facebook, Instagram 'Addictive' Under DSA](https://www.theverge.com/policy/963872/meta-eu-addictive-design-200b-fine-risk-digital-services-act-dsa) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The European Commission preliminarily found Meta in breach of the Digital Services Act over the addictive design of Facebook and Instagram, citing features like personalized recommendations, autoplay, and infinite scroll. Meta may face a fine up to $12 billion and be forced to redesign both apps. This is the first major DSA enforcement targeting addictive design, setting a precedent for how social media platforms must assess and mitigate risks to users' mental health. It signals stricter EU scrutiny of AI-driven recommendation systems and could reshape global compliance standards. The potential fine is up to 6% of Meta's worldwide annual turnover, which in 2025 was $200.97 billion, thus a maximum of $12 billion. Separately, the EU is considering a blocwide ban on social media for under-16s, and Meta faces a US trial in August over alleged intentional addictiveness.
> **Reason**: 该事件涉及AI推荐系统引发的监管行动，可能对社交平台的设计和AI应用合规产生重大影响，罚款金额巨大，且对行业有示范效应。
