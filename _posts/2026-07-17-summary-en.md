## [Kimi K3: World's First Open 3T-Class Model](https://www.kimi.com/blog/kimi-k3) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Kimi released K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-source model with native vision and a 1-million-token context window, the first open model to reach 3T scale. Kimi K3 pushes the frontier of open-source AI, offering near-frontier performance to developers and enterprises, potentially reshaping the competitive landscape against proprietary models. K3 uses Mixture of Experts with 16 active out of 896 experts, achieving 2.5x scaling efficiency over K2 via Kimi Delta Attention and Attention Residuals. It trails Claude Fable 5 and GPT 5.6 Sol but outperforms other tested models.
> **Reason**: 官方发布全新2.8T参数开源模型，具备原生视觉和百万token上下文，社区讨论热烈，影响开源模型格局。

## [AI Music Video Showdown: Claude Fable 5 vs GPT-5.6 Sol](https://www.tryai.dev/blog/ai-music-video-arena-claude-vs-gpt-5.6) ⭐️ 8.0/10

An experiment compared Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol in autonomously producing a music video with a $25 or $100 budget, logging every tool call. Both models completed a full-length video for "Uptown Funk" using a custom agentic harness with six tools. This head-to-head reveals how frontier models differ in tool utilization during open-ended, long-horizon tasks—critical for AI agent design. The experiment provides a reproducible benchmark for agentic harness behavior and cost efficiency across models. GPT-5.6 Sol used image-to-video at $25 and mixed three video models at $100, while Claude Fable 5 stuck to text-to-video. Token costs for Claude Fable 5 ($16.99–$25.05) were much higher than GPT-5.6 Sol ($3–$4), despite similar video generation spend.

## [CNKI to delist papers listing DeepSeek as author, citing copyright issues](https://www.zaobao.com.sg/news/china/story20260716-9371836) ⭐️ 8.0/10

China National Knowledge Infrastructure (CNKI) announced it will remove papers that list AI models like DeepSeek and Gemini as co-authors, citing that AI lacks civil subject qualification and cannot hold copyrights. This marks the first major Chinese academic platform to explicitly ban AI authorship, setting a precedent for academic publishing norms and copyright enforcement in the AI era. CNKI stated that authors must be natural persons, legal persons, or unincorporated organizations, and that AI tools can only be used as assistive tools, with disclosure required in the methodology or acknowledgments.
> **Reason**: 知网首次明确禁止AI列为论文作者，涉及AI学术规范、版权和伦理，对学术出版和AI使用具有重要政策指导意义，引发业界广泛关注。

## [Japan to Buy 27,500 NVIDIA Rubin Chips for Robot Sovereign AI](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/japan-to-buy-nvidia-rubin-chips-to-build-sovereign-ai-for-robots) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Japan announced plans to purchase 27,500 NVIDIA Rubin chips and build a large-scale data center led by new company Noetra, aiming to develop a sovereign AI model for robotics with a government grant of approximately 387.3 billion yen ($2.4 billion). This marks a major sovereign AI push by a G7 nation, directly challenging U.S. and Chinese dominance in AI and robotics. It signals a strategic shift where governments invest heavily in proprietary AI infrastructure to reduce foreign dependency and capture future robotics market share. Noetra, led by President Hiroshi Taba, targets releasing its first AI model next March and a robot-specific version within years. The project involves SoftBank, Preferred Networks (backed by Toyota), and NEC. Japan aims to capture over 30% of the global robotics market by 2040.
